<objective>
Implement the security-critical core of validated opts: a closed `DocOpts` struct with strict parsing and applicability checks (OPTS-01), a PDF/A-2b filter-options builder assembled purely from server-side constants (Pitfall 9 — the milestone's single highest-severity attack surface), the worker-side threading of stored opts into the converter, and a PDF/A OutputIntent output check that fails terminally when the produced PDF is not actually tagged (OPTS-02, D-05/D-06).

Purpose: This is where client-controlled bytes are prevented from ever reaching the `soffice` argv/filter-JSON, and where a regressed LibreOffice that silently returns a plain PDF under a PDF/A request is caught. The injection unit test here is the mandatory artifact for success criterion 1.

Output: New `internal/convert/opts.go`; `internal/convert/libreoffice.go`, `internal/convert/libreoffice_test.go`, and `internal/worker/worker.go` modified. The OutputIntent terminal-error string and its `terminalLibreOfficeSignatures` entry land in this same plan (D-06).
</objective>

| converter output → job status | LibreOffice output crosses into the done/failed decision; a mis-tagged PDF/A must not be reported as a successful archival export. |
